Baccalaureate (2nd Year) English

Master advanced English for the national exam. Deep grammar, rich vocabulary, analytical reading, and exam-ready writing skills.

Explore units →

What You'll Learn

🎤 Advanced Speaking

Debate current issues, express opinions with nuance, and respond spontaneously in exam conditions.

📖 Critical Reading

Analyze authentic texts, identify author's purpose, infer meaning, and answer exam-style comprehension questions.

✍️ Exam Writing

Write well-structured essays, formal letters, book reviews, and reports - all aligned with the national exam format.

Complete Bac Syllabus - 10 Thematic Units

  • Unit 1: Gifts of Youth

    You learn about youth talents and qualities, how to give opinions, use gerunds and infinitives, read about young people’s abilities, and describe a person.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 2: Humor

    You learn vocabulary about humor, how to make requests, use present and past modals, understand jokes and humor texts, and write a narrative paragraph.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 3: Education

    You learn education vocabulary, how to express purpose, use past perfect tenses, understand education texts, and write a report about an event.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 4: Sustainable Development

    You learn sustainable development vocabulary, how to express cause and effect, use future perfect tenses, read about environment topics, and write formal letters.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 5: Women and Power

    You learn women's rights vocabulary, suffix word formation, expressing addition and concession, passive voice, reading about women’s power, and writing a book review.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 6: Cultural Values

    You learn cultural values vocabulary, how to complain and apologize, use phrasal verbs, read cultural texts, and write informal/personal letters.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 7: Citizenship

    You learn citizenship vocabulary, how to give advice, use reported speech, understand citizenship texts, and fill application forms.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 8: International Organizations

    You learn organizations vocabulary and acronyms, how to respond to good/bad news, use linking words, read NGO topics, and write essays and formal emails.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 9: Science & Technology

    You learn science and technology vocabulary, how to express certainty/uncertainty, review conditionals (type 2 & 3), read tech texts, and write advantages/disadvantages essays.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

  • Unit 10: Brain Drain

    You learn brain drain vocabulary, prefixes, how to express regret, relative clauses (defining/non-defining), read about migration issues, and write essays or paragraphs.

    • Vocabulary
    • Grammar
    • Reading
    • Writing

Each unit includes vocabulary flashcards, interactive grammar exercises, reading comprehension tasks, and a mini quiz aligned with the national exam format.
