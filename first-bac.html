First Year Baccalaureate English

Bridge the gap to Baccalaureate success. Strong grammar, rich vocabulary, analytical reading, and structured writing for first year Bac students.

Explore units →

What You'll Learn

🎤 Speaking & Interaction

Express opinions, give advice, make suggestions, and discuss cultural, social, and environmental topics confidently.

📚 Advanced Reading

Analyze longer texts, extract main ideas, infer meaning, and respond critically to articles and stories.

✍️ Paragraphs & Essays

Write well-structured paragraphs, personal letters, formal letters, and short essays with coherence and correct punctuation.

Complete Syllabus - 10 Thematic Units

  • Unit 1: Our Cultural Heritage

    You learn about culture, how to ask and give opinions, use irregular verbs and articles, and write with correct punctuation and capitalization.

    • Vocabulary: Culture
    • Grammar: Irregular Verbs
    • Function: Ask & Give Opinion
    • Writing

  • Unit 2: Education

    You learn school vocabulary, how to give and ask for advice, past simple vs past continuous, “used to” for past habits, and how to write a personal letter.

    • Vocabulary: School
    • Grammar: Past Simple & Continuous
    • Grammar: Used to
    • Function
    • Writing: Personal Letter

  • Unit 3: Media

    You learn types of media and media jobs, how to express surprise and interest, use the present perfect, and write paragraphs and essays.

    • Vocabulary: Media
    • Grammar: Present Perfect
    • Function: Expressing Surprise & Interest
    • Writing: Paragraph vs Essay

  • Unit 4: Leisure & Entertainment

    You learn leisure activities and entertainment places, participle adjectives (-ed / -ing), expressing feelings, future forms (will / going to), too/enough, prepositions (in/on/at), and writing informal emails.

    • Vocabulary: Leisure
    • Grammar: Future
    • Grammar: Participle Adjectives
    • Grammar: Prepositions
    • Grammar: Too & Enough
    • Function: Expressing Feelings & Emotions
    • Writing: Informal Email

  • Unit 5: Celebrations

    You learn vocabulary about celebrations, how to make promises, relative pronouns (who/which/that/where/whose), and passive voice.

    • Vocabulary: Celebrations
    • Function: Expressing Promise
    • Grammar: Passive Voice
    • Grammar: Relative Pronouns

  • Unit 6: Health & Wellness

    You learn body parts and health problems, how to make complaints and apologies, conditionals (zero & first), and modal verbs (permission, obligation, possibility).

    • Vocabulary: Health
    • Grammar: Modals
    • Grammar: Conditional 1
    • Function: Complaining & Apologies

  • Unit 7: Ecology & Environment

    You learn ecological issues, word formation, expressing hopes and wishes, conditional type 2, and writing formal letters.

    • Vocabulary: Ecological Issues
    • Grammar: Noun-Verb-Adjective
    • Grammar: Conditional 2
    • Function: Hope & Wish

  • Unit 8: Society & Social Problems

    You learn social problems and solutions, how to make suggestions, phrasal verbs, and writing paragraphs vs essays.

    • Vocabulary: Social Problems
    • Grammar: Phrasal Verbs
    • Function: Making Suggestions
    • Writing: Paragraph & Essay

  • Unit 9: Human Rights

    You learn human rights problems, suffixes, how to apologize, reported speech (part 1), and universal human rights.

    • Vocabulary: Human Rights Problems
    • Grammar: Suffixes
    • Grammar: Reported Speech
    • Function: Expressing Apology

  • Unit 10: Travel & Tourism

    You learn tourism vocabulary, vacation activities and types, expressing possibility in present/past, and reported speech (said/asked/told).

    • Vocabulary: Travel
    • Grammar: Present & Past Possibility
    • Grammar: Reported Speech 2

Each unit includes vocabulary flashcards, interactive grammar exercises, reading comprehension tasks, and a mini quiz to track your progress.

Why First Year Baccalaureate Matters

The first year of the Baccalaureate serves as a solid foundation that prepares students for the second year, which is the final and most important stage of the program, where they take the regional exam at the end of the school year.
